№ 2 · The Nebula Score

Six seconds of full thrust.

A quick engine test for your chip: one core at full power, then all of them. Pure math — the same kind of work a game creates, over in seconds. The app reads iOS's thermal sensor before and during the run and simply refuses to start if your device is already warm. Nothing on your phone is touched, written or changed; you just learn what it can do.

3.1The two buttons that use the internet

The speed test. When you tap "Run test", the app exchanges randomized test traffic with Cloudflare's public measurement service (speed.cloudflare.com) to time your connection. Like any server you connect to, Cloudflare sees your IP address for the duration of the test; the traffic itself is meaningless random bytes, and the app sends no personal information — it has none to send. Results are saved only on your device and deleted after 7 days. Cloudflare's own privacy policy governs their side of the exchange.

Fetch public IP. When you tap it, the app asks a public echo service (api.ipify.org) what your internet-facing address is and shows you the answer. That's the entire transaction. The address is displayed, not stored.

3.2What lives on your device

Your settings, your speed-test history (last 7 days, pruned automatically) and your best Nebula Score are stored in the app's own container on your device. Delete the app and all of it is gone — we could not recover it for you even if you asked, because we never had it.

3.3Children

Nebula Stats collects nothing from anyone, and therefore nothing from children. There is no chat, no user content, and nothing to buy.

3.4If this policy ever changes

Any revision appears on this page with a new effective date before the update ships. There is no lower rung than "nothing collected", so a change could only describe a new feature's exact network behavior — as section 3.1 does today.
